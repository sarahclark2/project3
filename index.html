Climate Change: Is it a Myth or a Reality?

By Sarah Clark

Nov. 7, 2018

CHAPEL HILL F T M S

Rising temperatures. Increasing levels of carbon in the atmosphere. What does this mean for us? For nature? For the world as we know it? It means a lot.

Over the past couple of decades, we have seen an increase in average land temperature, 2016 being the hottest year on record. The graph above, with data provided by NASA shows the steady increase in temperature. Carbon dioxide, among other greenhouse gases, have contributed greatly to the increase of temperature on Earth.

Due to the rise in temperature, ice caps have been steadily melting. Why is this an issue? If you live on the coast, you may know why. The run-off from the melting ice-caps goes to the ocean, which means there has also been a steady increase in the rise of the sea-level. This is a huge threat to people living on island nations, or near the coast. This could potentially displace millions, as homes may be eventually consumed by the ocean. The graph below, with data available on NASA's website and sourced from the NOAA shows the increasing amount of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere.

Increasing levels in carbon also means increasing levels of toxicity in the air. The table above, with data provided by the EPAshows the air quality index in each county of the United States during 2017. Although toxicity in the air poses a much larger threat in big cities, such as Los Angeles, it is likely that this will continue to grow worse. Development and industrializing has been the trend for many years now as it brings wealth and value to different areas. It is also trend that probably will not stop very soon.
